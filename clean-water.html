Clean Water Initiative

I participated in a United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDG) engineering challenge, contributing to a solution that addresses SDG 14.1: reducing marine pollution. Our team designed the MACH 1, a fully mechanical marine waste compactor aimed at small to medium-sized boats.

Project Overview

Marine vessels contribute over 20% of global plastic pollution. Many sailors dispose of waste overboard due to lack of space and odor concerns. The MACH 1 solves this by providing an efficient, compact waste management solution that:

  • Reduces waste volume by 66% using a 3:1 compaction ratio
  • Operates without electricity via a hand-cranked scissor jack system
  • Eliminates odor through air compression and sealed liners
  • Resists corrosion with marine-grade Aluminum 6061-T6 and protective coatings

My Role

I was responsible for the 3D CAD modeling and mechanical part drawings of the MACH 1. I created detailed component schematics including:

  • The internal scissor jack compaction mechanism
  • Custom gear system for torque transmission
  • Removable bin liner and waste compartment
  • Gearbox housing, crankshaft integration, and bearing alignment

Engineering Highlights

Scissor Jack System

The hand-cranked scissor jack uses a 1 cm lead screw pitch with 20 cm arms, achieving a mechanical advantage of 20. This allows users to generate over 22kN of force with minimal effort.

Gear Transmission

The gearbox uses a 15:30 spur gear ratio in a parallel shaft configuration. A flexible shaft transmits torque from the crank to the lead screw while compensating for vertical motion of the jack. Human power input (~50W) converts into ~8Nm torque, well above the required 1.75Nm for compaction.

Material Selection

  • Internal Bin & Plate: Aluminum 6061-T6 (yield strength ≥ 244 MPa, corrosion resistant, lightweight)
  • Outer Housing: Same material with 2 mm wall thickness for stiffness and safety
  • Lid Plate: 10 mm thick aluminum plate with a 3.3x safety factor against maximum stress

Impact Resistance & Durability

Designed to survive 1-meter drops with full waste load (~51kg). Design considerations included shock distribution, retention ring-supported gear shafts, and bearing-mounted supports to reduce vibration and misalignment.

Outcomes & Impact

The MACH 1 presents a novel, compact waste management solution for the marine sector. It’s 6.7x lighter than industrial competitors, requires no electronics, and offers affordable, robust functionality tailored for yachts and small boats.

This project helped me apply mechanical design theory to real-world sustainability problems, blending CAD, gear mechanics, and stakeholder-centered engineering. It also showcased how AI tools like ChatGPT can support decision-making during prototyping and materials analysis.
